Who are the Ultramarines?

The XIII Legion: Guardians of the Five Hundred Worlds

The Ultramarines were forged during the Great Crusade as the thirteenth of the original twenty Space Marine Legions. While other Legions were defined by their savagery or their specialized psychic powers, the XIII was defined by logistics and statecraft. They didn't just conquer planets; they rebuilt them. Under the leadership of their Primarch, Roboute Guilliman, the Ultramarines established the Realm of Ultramar—a sub-empire of five hundred star systems centered around their fortress-world, Macragge. Unlike the rest of the galaxy's decaying feudal systems, Ultramar was a beacon of meritocracy and efficiency, proving that humanity could thrive through order rather than just survive through fear.

In the current era of the 41st Millennium, the Ultramarines are the "Standard-Bearers" of the Imperium. While the rest of the galaxy has descended into religious fanaticism and technological decay, the Ultramarines still cling to the Imperial Truth: the belief that logic, discipline, and duty are the only tools capable of holding back the xenos, the mutant, and the heretic. To an Ultramarine, a battle is a mathematical equation where Courage and Honor are the constants required to achieve victory.

To take a town, send a legionary. To take a city, send a squad. To take a world, send a company. To take a culture, send a Chapter.
